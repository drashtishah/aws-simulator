  Failure chain:
    1. Northwind backfill drives ~600,000 TPM (15% of account quota)
    2. 86 other tenants doing normal traffic add ~3.2M TPM
    3. Account TPM utilization climbs to 98%
    4. Bursts above 4M TPM/min get ThrottlingException
    5. Northwind rarely throttles (steady stream); other tenants take the hit
       on the bursts when their requests happen to land at peak
    6. Tenant clients see 429 from invoke-model Lambda; gateway throttle
       counters all zero; cause is one layer below the gateway

  Two-stage fix:
    Stage 1 (immediate):
      - Pause Northwind backfill via control channel
      - Account TPM falls to 65%; throttling stops
      - Tenant 5xx rates return to baseline within 5 minutes

    Stage 2 (structural):
      a. Token-aware per-tenant rate limiter in invoke-model Lambda:
         sliding 60-second window of tokens consumed per tenant_id,
         stored in DAX-fronted DynamoDB; reject locally above per-tenant
         cap (e.g., 400,000 TPM)
      b. Cross-region inference profile (us-east-1 + us-west-2 + us-east-2)
         to triple effective capacity without code change
      c. Northwind moved to Provisioned Throughput (15 model units,
         dedicated 750k TPM) so future backfills do not contend with
         on-demand at all
      d. CloudWatch alarms on EstimatedTPMQuotaUsage > 70% (warn) and
         > 90% (page); InvocationThrottles > 0 (page)
